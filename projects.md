## LLM as a Judge — Research Paper

- **Paper:** *Self-Preference Bias in LLM Juries: A Controlled Test of Hard Holdout* (Samuel Eder, JKU Linz, 2026)
- File: `research_and_papers/Self-Preference_Bias_in_LLM_Juries-A_Controlled_Test_of_Hard_Holdout.pdf`
- Curated a dataset of 4,196 individual judge votes across a 7-model jury spanning distinct providers
- Used 300 Chatbot Arena pairwise matchups as evaluation input
- Designed and ran controlled experiments comparing inclusive vs. hard-holdout jury configurations
- Introduced jury-level false endorsement rate (FER) as primary outcome metric
- Applied Wataoka's Equal Opportunity diagnostic for vote-level self-preference analysis
- Main finding: no detectable jury-level FER benefit from hard holdout (p = 0.875), despite clear vote-level self-preference in 6 of 7 models — separating vote-level bias from jury-level effect
